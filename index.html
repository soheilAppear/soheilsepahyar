About

My background combines experimental VR research and production-minded software development. In my Ph.D. work at Michigan Technological University, I studied distance perception in VR and built custom algorithms and tooling to process and analyze high-frequency behavioral data. In industry (Visteon), I applied ML, data engineering, and systems integration skills to reduce engineering cycle time and improve repeatability.

What I enjoy building
  • Gaze-contingent rendering systems with stable, testable parameterizations
  • Simulation and data collection pipelines that produce training-ready datasets
  • Tools that replace error-prone manual workflows with fast, reproducible automation
